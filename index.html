Biography

Interests and quirks.

Avery Laird is an experimental artist living in Vancouver, BC. He is willing to try just about any medium, style or technique but is most comfortable with typography, electric art and graphic design. His most enjoyable work is accomplished through a happy marriage of his electrical knowledge and interest with his eye for design. In the future, Avery hopes to continue to push the defined limits of visual art.

Gallery

Photography, Typography and Visual Design.

Panorama (above) was a photograph taken at Elfin Lakes in Garibaldi.

Sweater (upper right) is an example of perspective distortion due to extreme close-up.

Things (right) is an experiment of typography. The original text was completed on paper, then scanned. Once digital, I manipulated the colour and duplicated the image over four tiles to create an almost Andy Warhol-esque piece.

Fuzzy (below) a close up taken directly into the sun brought the lens of the camera into focus. Note the tiny scratches and dust on the lens.


Promises (left). Similar to "Things", this piece was completed on paper and then digitized.

Keypad (below). One of my early experiments with LEDs and light diffusion. Powered by an arduino and PWM (pulse width modulation).

Bracelet (below). Some of my wood work. Made all with hand tools: absolutely no power tools were used.
